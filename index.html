Italian Phrases & Quick Reference

Pronunciation tip: Roll your R's lightly, stress the next-to-last syllable in most words (CAff-AY, ris-tohr-AHNT-ay).

Greetings & Politeness

  • Buongiorno — Good morning (use until ~1 PM)
  • Buonasera — Good evening (after ~5 PM)
  • Grazie — Thank you
  • Grazie mille — Thank you very much
  • Per favore — Please
  • Scusi / Scusa — Excuse me (polite / informal)
  • Mi dispiace — I'm sorry
  • Prego — You're welcome
  • Arrivederci — Goodbye
  • Ciao — Bye (informal, use with people you know)

  • Vino rosso / bianco — Red wine / White wine

Numbers (quick ref for dining, shopping, asking times)

  • 1 = uno · 2 = due · 3 = tre · 4 = quattro · 5 = cinque
  • 6 = sei · 7 = sette · 8 = otto · 9 = nove · 10 = dieci
  • 20 = venti · 30 = trenta · 40 = quaranta · 50 = cinquanta · 100 = cento
Pro tip: Italians appreciate effort even if your pronunciation isn't perfect. A "Buongiorno" and smile at a shop or restaurant goes a long way. If you get stuck, point, smile, and say "Non parlo italiano" — most people in tourist areas speak some English or will try to help.
